# "Take 3 on the Bounce" Strategies

In voiceover work, "doing it on the bounce" means performing multiple takes of the same line back-to-back in a single recording, often with slight variations in delivery.

When asked to generate a "Take 3", use one of the following approaches by structuring the Transcript section with explicit "Take" markers and using Markup Tags or direct textual cues to guide the virtual actor.

Ensure you use `[short pause]` or `[medium pause]` between takes to give the audio realistic separation.

## Approach 1: The "Safety" Set
Use this when the core emotion is correct, but you want slight variations in polish or emphasis.

* **Take 1 (The Direct Read):** The most straightforward interpretation of the script.
* **Take 2 (The Tweak):** A slight change in inflection or emphasis on a specific word.
* **Take 3 (The Safe Option):** A conservative, highly polished version ensuring technical accuracy.

**Transcript Example Structure:**
```markdown
#### TRANSCRIPT
[short pause]
Take 1. The Direct Read.
[short pause]
Welcome to the future of cloud computing. We are excited to have you here.
[medium pause]

Take 2. Emphasize "future".
[short pause]
Welcome to the *future* of cloud computing. [short pause] We are excited to have you here.
[medium pause]

Take 3. The Safe Option. Slower and polished.
[short pause]
Welcome to the future of cloud computing. We are excited to have you here.
```

## Approach 2: The Character/Emotional Shift
Use this when exploring different facets of a character's reaction to a situation. Make sure the three takes sound distinct from one another.

* **Take 1 (The Initial Idea):** Your best interpretation of the character's primary emotion.
* **Take 2 (The Contrast):** An opposite, extreme version (e.g., angry vs. pleading).
* **Take 3 (The Wild Card):** A completely different, unexpected take exploring a new angle.

**Transcript Example Structure:**
```markdown
#### TRANSCRIPT
[short pause]
Take 1. The Initial Idea. Frustrated.
[short pause]
[sigh] I can't believe the build failed again.
[medium pause]

Take 2. The Contrast. Defeated and sad.
[short pause]
[whispering] I can't believe the build failed again.
[medium pause]

Take 3. The Wild Card. Sarcastic amusement.
[short pause]
[laughing] Oh great, I can't believe the build failed *again*. Fantastic.
```

## Approach 3: The Pacing/Emphasis Variation
Use this for commercial reads, marketing copy, or high-energy scripts where rhythm is crucial.

* **Take 1 (Upbeat/Fast):** High energy, quick tempo.
* **Take 2 (Relaxed/Slow):** A more conversational, slower approach.
* **Take 3 (Punchy):** Short, focused emphasis on key words.

**Transcript Example Structure:**
```markdown
#### TRANSCRIPT
[short pause]
Take 1. Upbeat and Fast.
[short pause]
[extremely fast] Get yours today before they're entirely sold out!
[medium pause]

Take 2. Relaxed and Slow.
[short pause]
Get yours today... [short pause] before they're entirely sold out.
[medium pause]

Take 3. Punchy.
[short pause]
Get yours *today*! [short pause] Before they're sold out!
```
